For the extensions, I think they are all really interesting but I would probably start with agglomeration since this is the only one that wouldn't change the equilibrium of the static payoff game.

I also looked at the Igami paper and didn't see a good explanation for how he solved the game (it's not quite as easy as you wrote up since you have to iterate the backward and forward induction until they converge). Allan Hsiao's thesis has a fairly good explanation of this in the appendix if you need it. I would also suggest starting with just 2 periods to make things as simple as possible and then you can move forward from there.
